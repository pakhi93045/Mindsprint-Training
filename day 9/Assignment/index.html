1. Bangalore Palace

Inspired by England's Windsor Castle, Bangalore Palace boasts Tudor-style architecture with fortified towers, arches, and lush green lawns. Built in 1887, this majestic palace offers a glimpse into the opulent lifestyle of the Wadiyar dynasty.

2. Lalbagh Botanical Garden

Spread over 240 acres, Lalbagh Botanical Garden is a haven for nature lovers. Home to a diverse range of flora, centuries-old trees, and a stunning glasshouse, it's the perfect place for a peaceful stroll or a family picnic.

3. Cubbon Park

Located in the heart of the city, Cubbon Park is a sprawling green space ideal for morning jogs, leisurely walks, and picnics. The park is dotted with beautiful statues, historical buildings, and a variety of flora.

Bangalore Palace

History

Built in 1887, Bangalore Palace was constructed by Rev. J. Garrett, who was the first Principal of Central High School in Bangalore, now known as Central College. The palace was bought by the then Maharaja of Mysore, Chamaraja Wadiyar X.

Architecture

The palace is designed in Tudor-style architecture featuring:

  • Fortified towers
  • Gothic windows
  • Battlement-like parapets
  • Elegant woodcarvings

Visitor Information

  • Opening Hours: 10:00 AM - 5:30 PM
  • Entry Fee: ₹230 for Indians, ₹460 for foreigners
  • Camera Fee: Additional ₹300
  • Audio Guide: Available in multiple languages

Lalbagh Botanical Garden

History

Lalbagh was established by Hyder Ali in 1760 and later extended by his son Tipu Sultan. The garden was developed to be a private garden for the royal family.

Attractions

  • The Famous Glass House
  • The Lalbagh Lake
  • The Lalbagh Rock (one of the oldest rock formations on earth)
  • The Bonsai Garden
  • The Topiary Park

Visitor Information

  • Opening Hours: 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM
  • Entry Fee: ₹20 for adults
  • Best Time to Visit: Early morning or late afternoon
  • Famous for bi-annual flower shows

Cubbon Park

History

Established in 1870, Cubbon Park was created by Richard Sankey, the then Chief Engineer of Mysore. It was named after Sir Mark Cubbon, the longest-serving commissioner of Mysore.

Attractions

  • State Central Library
  • Bangalore Aquarium
  • Children's Play Area
  • Statues of Famous Personalities
  • Various Government Buildings

Visitor Information

  • Opening Hours: 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM
  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Best for: Morning walks, jogging, photography
  • Special events on weekends
